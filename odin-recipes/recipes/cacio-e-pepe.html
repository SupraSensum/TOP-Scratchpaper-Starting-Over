Cacio e Pepe

Description

Cacio e Pepe is a Roman pasta dish that translates to "cheese and pepper" â€” a testament to its simple, minimalist ingredients. Made with spaghetti or tonnarelli, Pecorino Romano cheese, freshly cracked black pepper, and starchy pasta water, it forms a silky, peppery sauce without any butter or cream.

Its brilliance lies in the technique: tossing the hot pasta and grated cheese vigorously until a creamy emulsion forms. The result is a deeply flavorful dish that celebrates balance, texture, and tradition with just a few pantry staples.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup spaghetti
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Boil spaghetti in salted water until al dente.
  2. Reserve some pasta water before draining.
  3. Melt butter in a pan over low heat.
  4. Add Parmesan cheese and stir until melted, adding pasta water if needed.
  5. Season with black pepper and serve.
