Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian comfort food made by layering wide sheets of pasta with rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and a blend of melted cheeses. Baked until bubbly and golden, it offers a perfect balance of savory, cheesy, and hearty flavors in every bite. The dish is traditionally made with beef or pork, though variations include vegetables or alternative proteins.

Each layer soaks up flavor from the sauces, creating a melt-in-your-mouth texture that’s deeply satisfying. Often served at family gatherings and celebrations, lasagna is a timeless favorite that brings warmth and indulgence to the table.

Ingredients

  • 8-10 no-boil lasagna noodles
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 jar marinara sauce (24 oz)
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Cook ground beef in a skillet until browned, then add marinara sauce and simmer for a few minutes.
  2. Mix ricotta cheese with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  3. In a 9x13 inch baking dish, layer marinara sauce, lasagna noodles, ricotta mixture, and mozzarella cheese. Repeat this process two more times.
  4. Finish with a layer of noodles, marinara sauce, and mozzarella cheese on top. Cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes.
  5. Remove foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is golden brown.
